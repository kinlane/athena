Google is aiming to fight back against so-called patent trolls, announcing Thursday on its Open Source blog that its taking part in a self-designed Open Patent Non-Assertion Pledge (OPN).Going forward, this means that the company will take 10 of its patents and pledge not to sue any user, distributor or developer of open-source software on [those] specified patents, unless first attacked.Google goes on to explain what it means by first attacked: The Pledge may be terminated, but only if a party brings a patent suit against Google products or services, or is directly profiting from such litigation.The tech giant also says that it will expand the number of patents included in the pledge over time, and urges other tech companies to join in on the initiative.We hope the OPN Pledge will serve as a model for the industry, the post says.
